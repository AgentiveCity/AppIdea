# Gamified STEM Learning App for Kids

## Overview
A gamified learning app teaching science, technology, engineering, and math to kids (ages 6-14) through interactive games and challenges. Minecraft meets Khan Academy.

## Market Opportunity
- **Market Size**: K-12 STEM education $60.14B growing at 13.7% annually
- **Problem**: Kids think STEM is boring, dropout rates high
- **Success**: Prodigy (100M+ kids), Khan Academy Kids (20M+)

## Key Features
1. **Game-Based Learning**: Learn by playing, not lectures
2. **Adaptive Difficulty**: AI adjusts to child's level
3. **Progress Rewards**: Unlock characters, worlds
4. **Parent Dashboard**: Track learning progress
5. **Multiplayer Challenges**: Compete with friends
6. **Curriculum-Aligned**: Matches school standards

## Revenue Model
- **Free**: Basic content, ads
- **Premium**: $9.99/month - Ad-free, all content, reports
- **Family**: $14.99/month - Up to 4 kids

**Path to $100K MRR**: 10,000 Premium subscribers @ $9.99 = $100K

## Viral Mechanics
- **Referral Rewards**: In-game currency for invites
- **Leaderboards**: Kids compete, invite friends
- **Achievement Sharing**: Parents share milestones

## Competitive Advantages
- **Fun-First**: More engaging than Khan Academy Kids
- **STEM-Focused**: Not general education (niche advantage)
- **Social Features**: Multiplayer (competitors single-player)

## Go-to-Market
1. **Parent Communities**: Mom Facebook groups
2. **Schools**: Freemium model for classrooms
3. **YouTube Kids**: Educational gaming content
